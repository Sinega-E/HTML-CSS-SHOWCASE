An accordion is a user interface (UI) component that allows content to be shown and hidden interactively. It consists of sections, where clicking on a section header expands its associated content while collapsing others. Accordions are commonly used to organize and display large amounts of content in a compact, readable format.

Accordions are commonly used in the following scenarios: FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions): To allow users to expand and view answers to specific questions. Content Navigation: For categorizing and collapsing content, such as in documentation or dashboards. Forms: To separate form fields into sections, showing one part at a time. Product Details: To hide or reveal specifications, reviews, or additional information in e-commerce websites.

Smooth transitions enhance the user experience by making the opening and closing of sections feel natural and intuitive. Abrupt changes can confuse users or make the interaction feel less polished.
